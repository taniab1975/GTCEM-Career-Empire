## Module Promise

Students learn that careers are not fixed, linear, or finished once school ends. Instead, they build their future through:

- continual learning
- pathway flexibility
- training choices
- reflection
- adaptation
- ongoing professional development

## Main Gameplay Loop

Each round should move through:

1. Identify a future barrier or learning need
2. Choose a learning or training response
3. Complete a short interactive task
4. Reflect on why the choice matters
5. Apply the learning to a pathway decision
6. Bank rewards into the shared Career Empire profile

## Module Chapters

### Chapter 1: What Is Lifelong Learning?

#### Learning focus

- define lifelong learning
- connect it to career development
- challenge the idea of one straight career path

#### Student fantasy

"My future does not have to be one fixed road. I can adapt and keep building."

#### Main mechanic

**Pathway Map Builder**

Students are shown one career goal and multiple possible routes to get there.

Example:

- direct university entry
- TAFE then work then university
- apprenticeship pathway
- job first then upskill later

#### Play sequence

1. Intro card:
   "Most careers are not linear."
2. Student sees one destination with four routes.
3. Student identifies which routes are realistic and why.
4. Student completes a mini-sort:
   `linear thinking` vs `lifelong learning thinking`
5. Student writes:
   "What is lifelong learning and why is it important?"

#### Assessment evidence

- short response:
  `What is lifelong learning and why is it important for career development?`

#### Shared rewards

- `career_success` +1
- `resilience` +1
- `communication` XP +4

#### Misconceptions to catch

- "Learning ends after school"
- "There is only one pathway into a career"
- "Changing direction means failure"

### Chapter 2: Learning to Learn

#### Learning focus

- motivation
- proactive learning
- learning habits
- choosing effective learning strategies

#### Main mechanic

**Learning Strategy Lab**

Students are given situations and must choose the strongest learning response.

Example situations:

- You do not understand a new task at work.
- You need to learn software quickly.
- You failed an assessment and need to improve.
- You want to prepare for a future pathway over the next 12 months.

#### Player actions

Choices include:

- watch a tutorial
- ask a mentor
- practise repeatedly
- research independently
- reflect and adjust
- ignore the problem

#### Play sequence

1. Student gets a learning challenge.
2. Student selects 2 best strategies out of 5.
3. Student completes a timed match activity:
   challenge -> best learning approach
4. Student justifies one choice in writing.

#### Assessment evidence

- justification:
  `Which learning strategy is best for this situation, and why?`

#### Shared rewards

- `time-management` XP +6
- `critical-thinking` XP +5
- `work_life_balance` +1 if planning choice is strong

#### Misconceptions to catch

- "Being good at learning is fixed"
- "Motivation is enough without strategy"
- "Asking for help means weakness"

### Chapter 3: Training and Development

#### Learning focus

- formal vs informal learning
- on-the-job vs off-the-job
- professional development
- micro-credentials

#### Main mechanic

**Training Sort Challenge**

Students classify examples into the correct training categories.

#### Classification buckets

- formal
- informal
- on-the-job
- off-the-job

#### Example cards

- apprenticeship
- TAFE certificate
- university degree
- mentoring
- supervision
- coaching
- watching an online tutorial
- workplace demonstration
- micro-credential
- WHS induction

#### Play sequence

1. Students drag cards into the correct category.
2. Game gives immediate feedback.
3. Then a scenario asks:
   `Which training option best fits this person’s next step?`

#### Assessment evidence

- explanation:
  `Why is this learning or training option the best fit for the scenario?`

#### Shared rewards

- `digital-literacy` XP +4
- `critical-thinking` XP +6
- `career_success` +1

#### Misconceptions to catch

- "Only formal training counts"
- "Informal learning is not real learning"
- "Professional development only matters for high-level jobs"

### Chapter 4: Pathways to Success

#### Learning focus

- multiple routes to one destination
- planning alternatives
- handling barriers
- adapting instead of stopping

#### Main mechanic

**Roadblock Navigator**

Students choose how to respond when the direct route to a goal is blocked.

#### Example roadblocks

- no apprenticeship offer yet
- low ATAR
- financial pressure
- lack of experience
- family responsibilities
- changing interests

#### Response choices

- wait and do nothing
- find a stepping-stone pathway
- get relevant experience
- complete a short course
- seek advice and revise the plan
- give up completely

#### Play sequence

1. Student chooses a career goal.
2. A barrier appears.
3. Student must build:
   - Plan A
   - Plan B
   - Plan C
4. Student explains why one alternate route is still valid.

#### Assessment evidence

- pathway plan:
  `Create a pathway with at least two alternatives if your first route is blocked.`

#### Shared rewards

- `resilience` +2
- `problem-solving` XP +6
- `career_success` +1

#### Misconceptions to catch

- "A setback means the career is over"
- "Alternative pathways are worse pathways"
- "Changing direction is going backwards"

### Chapter 5: Continuing Professional Development

#### Learning focus

- learning continues after qualification
- workers must update, specialise, and adapt
- CPD supports employability and progression

#### Main mechanic

**Career Maintenance Simulator**

Students take a qualified worker through 5-10 career years and decide how they maintain relevance.

#### Example careers

- electrician
- accountant
- nurse
- mechanic
- teacher
- IT technician

#### Example CPD choices

- compliance update
- technology retraining
- short course
- specialist credential
- mentoring
- leadership development
- safety refresher

#### Play sequence

1. Student chooses a worker profile.
2. Year-by-year changes appear:
   - technology shift
   - regulation change
   - leadership opportunity
   - skills gap
3. Student chooses the best CPD response.
4. Student finishes by explaining how that worker stays employable over time.

#### Assessment evidence

- explanation:
  `How would a worker in this occupation continue professional development throughout their career?`

#### Shared rewards

- `job_security` +2
- `salary` +1
- `critical-thinking` XP +4
- `time-management` XP +4

#### Misconceptions to catch

- "Once qualified, you are set for life"
- "Only struggling workers need retraining"
- "CPD is just paperwork"

## Suggested Teacher Interventions

### If Time Management is weak

- assign Chapter 2 replay
- provide scaffolded learning-plan template

### If Critical Thinking is weak

- revisit training classification
- compare two pathway options side by side

### If Communication is weak

- use sentence starters for reflection tasks
- provide model responses

### If Resilience is weak

- focus on Chapter 4 barriers and alternate routes
- reinforce that indirect paths are still valid

## Completion Rules

### Completion

A student completes the module when they:

- finish all five chapters
- submit all teacher-visible evidence tasks
- reach at least one viable pathway plan and one CPD explanation

### Mastery

A student is considered proficient when they:

- correctly classify learning/training types
- explain the purpose of lifelong learning clearly
- justify appropriate learning strategies
- build multiple realistic pathways
- explain CPD in a chosen career

### Extension

Students can go beyond completion by:

- designing a custom pathway for a chosen occupation
- comparing formal and informal learning options
- proposing their own micro-credential or development plan
